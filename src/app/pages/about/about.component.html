🎯 What is SDG 11?

Sustainable Development Goal 11 aims to "make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable" by 2030. It is one of the 17 global goals established by the United Nations in 2015.

With over 4.4 billion people (55% of the world's population) living in cities today — expected to reach 68% by 2050 — SDG 11 addresses critical urban challenges including inadequate housing, air pollution, traffic congestion, lack of public transport, and limited access to public services.

The goal has 10 targets covering housing, transport, disaster resilience, environmental impact, public spaces, and cultural heritage.

📋 SDG 11 Targets We Address

🚌

Target 11.2 — Accessible Transport

By 2030, provide access to safe, affordable, accessible, and sustainable transport systems for all, improving road safety and expanding public transport.

In Baguio City: Our app provides complete jeepney route information, modern PUJ details, and transport cooperative contacts to help residents choose public transport over private vehicles — reducing traffic and emissions.
🚨

Target 11.5 — Disaster Resilience

Significantly reduce the number of deaths and people affected by disasters, focusing on protecting the poor and vulnerable.

In Baguio City: Our app centralizes 24/7 emergency contacts for CDRRMO, BCPO, Fire Department, and BGHMC — enabling quick response during earthquakes, typhoons, landslides, and fires in all 128 barangays.
🏛️

Target 11.7 — Inclusive Public Services

Provide universal access to safe, inclusive, and accessible green and public spaces, particularly for women, children, elderly, and persons with disabilities.

In Baguio City: Our app connects all 128 barangays to City Hall services, DSWD assistance, PESO employment programs, and City Health Office — ensuring no one is left behind.

🌍 Baguio City's SDG Journey

Baguio City is a pioneer in the Philippines for localizing the Sustainable Development Goals. In partnership with the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP), the city completed its first Voluntary Local Review (VLR) of the SDGs in 2025 — making it one of the few cities in Southeast Asia to do so.

The VLR was developed through 18 months of participatory planning involving all 128 barangays, civil society organizations, youth groups, national agencies, and academic institutions including the University of Baguio.

The city's long-term vision, "Baguio 2043: A Livable, Inclusive, and Creative City", places SDG 11 at its core with commitments to sustainable urban development, disaster resilience, and inclusive public services.

⚠️ Why Baguio City Needs SDG 11

Baguio faces unique urbanization challenges that make SDG 11 critically important:

400,000+ Residents vs 30,000 ideal capacity
100,000+ Vehicles only 8,000 parking slots
300-400 Tons of waste/day strain on disposal system
1,000% Over Capacity Population exceeds limit

According to the city's Voluntary Local Review, accelerated efforts are needed to achieve the SDGs by 2030 — particularly in transportation accessibility, disaster preparedness, and inclusive access to government services.

📱 How Our App Supports SDG 11 in Baguio

  • 🚌 Transport Route Finder — Complete jeepney route information promoting public transport use (Target 11.2)
  • 🚨 Emergency Hotline — One-tap access to CDRRMO, police, fire, and hospital services (Target 11.5)

By making these services visible, accessible, and easy to use, we help Baguio residents navigate their city safely and efficiently — moving toward the vision of "No one is left behind".
